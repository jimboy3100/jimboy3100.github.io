🛡️ Anti-Teaming System — Discrete Events

This server uses a discrete event-based anti-teaming system. Instead of a single bucket that accumulates forever, every action is tracked as a separate event with its own lifespan. When an event expires, its penalty is removed — older actions don't get extended by newer ones.

Penalty Sources

Each of these actions adds a discrete anti-team event. All penalties are expressed as direct extra decay %/4s:

  • ☣ Virus (pop) — +1.6%/4s, lasts 2 minutes 30 seconds. Triggered when you eat a virus.
  • ◉➚◉ Split — +0.2%/4s, lasts 2 minutes 30 seconds. Triggered each time you split.
  • ⬤ W (eject mass) — +0.016%/4s per W press, lasts 1 minute 15 seconds. Flat per press — same penalty whether you have 1 cell or 16.
  • ⚠ Danger Zone — +0.4%/sec (flat per player), batched every 4 seconds, lasts 2 minutes 30 seconds. Only applies when the map is contracting and you have cells in the outer ring. 4 seconds in the danger zone = same penalty as one virus pop.
  • ∞ Constant Decay — always active, never expires. This is the natural mass loss. It's a flat percentage of your mass — a 10,000 mass cell loses ~80, a 100 mass cell loses ~0.8. The base rate scales dynamically with player count using a square-root curve: −0.8%/4s with ≤32 players (vanilla Agar.io rate, verified from 97,806 real packets), decreasing smoothly as more players join: 64 players → 0.57%, 128 → 0.40%, 256 → 0.28%, 512 → 0.20%, 1024 → 0.14%. Formula: decay = 0.8% × √(32 / players) when players > 32.

How It Works

Your total anti-team penalty is the sum of all currently active events (in %/4s). When the total exceeds the threshold (2.8%/4s), you start losing extra mass — but only the excess above the threshold is applied:

extra decay = max(0, total − 2.8%)

Example: You eat a virus (+1.6%) and split twice (+0.4%) = total 2.0%. Still below 2.8% threshold — no extra penalty. Add another virus pop (+1.6%) = total 3.6%. Extra = 3.6% − 2.8% = 0.8%/4s extra decay on top of your constant decay.

Key: Each event expires independently. If you eat a virus at t=0 and another at t=60, the first virus event expires at t=150 and the second at t=210. They don't extend each other. Once all events expire, your penalties return to 0 and decay goes back to normal.

Live Stats HUD — Reading the Display

When connected to Expanding Land FFA, you can see your live anti-team status in the stats bar. Here is an example of what it looks like:

⚗ Anti: (3.6%/2.8%, −1.6%/4s) | ☣ Virus: 2 (+3.2%, ⏳2m30s) | ◉➚◉ Split: 2 (+0.4%, ⏳2m20s) | ∞ −0.8%/4s

What each part means:

  • ⚗ Anti: (3.6%/2.8%, −1.6%/4s) — Your total penalty is 3.6%/4s (above the 2.8% threshold). The −1.6%/4s is your extra decay on top of constant decay (3.6% − 2.8% = 0.8%, plus constant). When below threshold, the score appears in green and no extra decay is shown.
  • ☣ Virus: 2 (+3.2%, ⏳2m30s) — 2 active virus events contributing +3.2%/4s (1.6% each). ⏳2m30s = the last virus event expires in 2m30s.
  • ◉➚◉ Split: 2 (+0.4%, ⏳2m20s) — 2 active split events contributing +0.4% (0.2% each).
  • ⬤ W: 5 (+0.08%, ⏳1m10s) — 5 active W events. Flat per press, not per cell.
  • ⚠ Zone 120s (+1.6%, ⏳2m30s) — You're in the danger zone (120s left in the phase). The danger zone has accumulated +1.6%/4s of penalty.
  • ∞ −X%/4s — The constant base decay. Always present. The rate depends on how many players are alive: 0.8%/4s with ≤32 players (vanilla), decreasing with more players (e.g. 0.57% at 64, 0.40% at 128, 0.28% at 256). This is the natural game decay and is independent of the anti-team system.

⏳ Important: "Last Expires" Explained

The ⏳ timer shows when the last (most recent) event of that type expires. It does NOT mean all events of that type expire at once.

Example: You eat a virus at t=0 and another at t=60. The display might show ☣ Virus: 2 (+3.2%, ⏳2m30s). The ⏳2m30s is the second virus's timer. The first virus actually expires at t=150 (90 seconds from now), while the second expires at t=210 (150 seconds from now). When the first expires, the display updates to ☣ Virus: 1 (+1.6%, ⏳1m30s).
